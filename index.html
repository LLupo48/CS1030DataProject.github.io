Kahn Academy Summaries

1.      Data Tools

The first module covers what tools can be used for data analysis. This module also briefly touches on what tools can be used for storage such as databases and the languages used to interact with them such as SQL. The quizzes also helped to know what questions can be answered with certain data sets.

2.      Big Data

This module covers big data. An interesting fact in the first unit of this module is that there are 175 zettabytes (175 billion terabytes) of digital data by 2025. This demonstrates that we will need to improve how data is stored and analyzed. The third unit in the module shows the importance of data centers in storing and analyzing big data.

3.      Bias in Machine Learning

The third module explores bias in machine learning. This topic is important as there are ethical concerns that arise when analyzing data. For example, this module covers the use of recidivism algorithms and how racial bias is present in analyzing data in this context. It is important to be aware of bias as bias can present unreliable or harmful results when analyzing data.

4.      Unit Test

The unit test module tests on elements that were taught in the previous 3 modules. The test is helpful for reinforcing how to interpret data sets and what questions can be answered given a data set. The test was also helpful by providing questions based on visual interpretations of data such as graphs. The unit test also incorporates how machine learning bias plays a role in analyzing data sets. Overall, this module was helpful in tying together all the concepts that were taught in the first 3 modules.

 

 

NFL Teams Value and Super Bowl Wins

Hypothesis/Question:

Does the monetary value of NFL teams correlate to Super Bowl championship wins?

Process:

Data sets from Statista.com were exported to excel and visualized in the graphs seen below. The data sets used were the Value of NFL Franchises in 2022 and most Super Bowl wins per team from 1967 (start of the “Super Bowl Era”) – 2023. These data sets were visualized into bar graphs and compared.

Analysis:

The top 5 teams in terms of revenue are shown to have won Super Bowl championships at least once. The bottom 5 teams in terms of revenue are shown to have never won a championship. This shows that at extreme ends, the correlation between revenue and championship wins is present.

This correlation is not present when analyzing teams such as the Pittsburgh Steelers. In 2022, the Steelers were ranked 17th in revenue (bottom half of the league) but are tied with the New England Patriots with 6 championship wins.

After analyzing these two data sets, it is difficult to conclude a solid correlation between revenue and Super Bowl wins. There are more confounding variables that contribute to the success of NFL teams such as quality of staff and performance of players. Another element that obfuscates this correlation is the amount of time that is covered by the Super Bowl era. With this considered, further analysis may benefit from data sets showing revenue by team during the years they had won a championship. For example, the Dallas Cowboys appear to be a very successful team with the most revenue in the league but have not won a championship since 1996. The introduction of a salary cap in the NFL (the amount teams can spend on players) also obfuscates the correlation of revenue and championship wins as in theory all teams are on an equal playing field in terms of spending.

In conclusion, the top and bottom teams show a correlation between revenue and championship wins, but there are many more confounding variables that go into an NFL team’s success.
